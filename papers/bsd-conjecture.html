Research Paper

From Local Height Diagonalization to Birch–Swinnerton–Dyer: Unconditional μ=0 and Cyclotomic IMC Equality

Jonathan Washburn

Recognition Science, Recognition Physics Institute, Austin, Texas, USA

Email: jon@recognitionphysics.org

Abstract

We prove the Birch–Swinnerton–Dyer conjecture unconditionally for every modular elliptic curve over ℚ by a classical, prime-wise route that converts local p-adic height information into global consequences and closes the cyclotomic Iwasawa Main Conjecture at all primes.

The core mechanism is a reduction-order separation criterion that upper-triangularizes the cyclotomic p-adic height Gram matrix modulo p, together with a per-prime diagonal unit test. We prove a Λ-adic positivity package (ordinary and signed) that yields reverse divisibility without invoking IMC, and a T=0 reverse-divisibility theorem forcing μₚ(E)=0 at primes passing the triangularization test.

Key Contributions

Separation Criterion

A reduction-order separation criterion that upper-triangularizes the cyclotomic p-adic height Gram matrix modulo p, enabling systematic analysis of height-unit primes.

Unconditional μ=0

Proves μₚ(E)=0 unconditionally for every prime p via a p-adic boundary wedge certificate on cyclotomic character layers.

Algorithmic Implementation

Converts abstract theory into concrete computational procedures with deterministic, portable, and auditable outputs.

Classical Methods

Uses transparent, auditable techniques rather than deep modern machinery, making verification practical.
