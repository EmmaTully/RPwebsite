The Meta-Principle Foundation

The entire framework begins with a single logical tautology: "Nothing cannot recognize itself." This isn't a physical assumption—it's a logical boundary condition. The formal statement is:

MP ≡ ¬∃r ∈ Recognition(∅,∅)

From this tautology alone, eight theorems follow necessarily, determining the unique structure that any self-consistent reality must have. The proof is constructive and requires no classical axioms.

Why This Matters

If absolute nothingness could somehow check on itself—could ask "am I nothing?"—then it would have structure: a checking mechanism, a question to ask, a state to verify. But having structure means it's not nothing anymore. This creates a logical trap: nothingness with self-awareness is a contradiction.

What MP tells us is simple: any reality that can verify its own existence must already be something, not nothing. This isn't philosophy—it's a logical boundary. And from this boundary, everything else follows necessarily.
