A boundary product–certificate proof of the Riemann Hypothesis

Jonathan Washburn

Independent Researcher

Abstract

We prove the Riemann Hypothesis via a single boundary route. A quantitative product certificate on \(\{\Re s>\tfrac12\}\) yields an almost‑everywhere boundary wedge (P+) for a normalized ratio; Poisson transport and a Cayley transform provide Schur/Herglotz control on zero‑free rectangles; a pinch across putative off‑critical zeros then globalizes the bound and eliminates such zeros. The right‑hand side uses only a local Cauchy–Riemann/Green pairing on Whitney boxes with a Carleson \(L^2\) bound for the Poisson extension. All load‑bearing steps are unconditional; diagnostic numerics are gated and do not enter the inequalities that close (P+) and the globalization.

Key Points

  • Boundary product–certificate yields quantitative wedge (P+) almost everywhere on the critical line.
  • Poisson transport and Cayley convert boundary positivity to Herglotz/Schur bounds in the interior.
  • Schur–Herglotz pinch removes putative off‑critical zeros; no adjustable parameters.
  • Right‑hand side uses only CR–Green pairing on Whitney boxes and Carleson energy bounds.
