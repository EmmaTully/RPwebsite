The Meta-Principle: A Tautological Foundation for Physics

Jonathan Washburn

We establish that "Nothing cannot recognize itself" serves as a tautological foundation for all physical law. Through formal logical analysis, we demonstrate that this simple statement necessitates the eight fundamental theorems of Recognition Physics, yielding parameter-free derivations of all physical constants and universal behaviors. The framework provides testable predictions with experimental accuracy exceeding current standard model precision while requiring zero free parameters or empirical fitting.

Parameter-Free Particle Masses from φ-Ladder Recognition Theory

Jonathan Washburn

Using the golden ratio φ-ladder framework derived from Recognition Physics, we predict 85+ particle masses with sub-percent accuracy. The universal mass formula m = B·E_coh·φ^(r+f) emerges from minimal ledger path analysis and RG flow integrals, providing exact values for fundamental particles without free parameters. We demonstrate precise matches to experimental masses for leptons, baryons, and mesons.
