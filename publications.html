From Local Height Diagonalization to Birch–Swinnerton–Dyer: Unconditional μ=0 and Cyclotomic IMC Equality via p-adic Wedge and Rigid Schur-Pinch

Jonathan Washburn

We prove the Birch–Swinnerton–Dyer conjecture unconditionally for every modular elliptic curve over ℚ by a classical, prime-wise route that converts local p-adic height information into global consequences. The method uses a separation criterion that upper-triangularizes the cyclotomic p-adic height Gram matrix modulo p, combined with Λ-adic positivity packages yielding reverse divisibility and establishing μₚ(E)=0 at every prime through systematic algorithmic procedures.

Keywords: Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture, p-adic height, Iwasawa theory, Selmer groups, elliptic curves

From a Logical Tautology to Eight Forced Theorems: The Recognition Science Derivation of Reality's Core Structure

Recognition Physics Institute

We show that a single logical tautology—the Meta-Principle (MP), "nothing cannot recognize itself"—forces eight core theorems (T1–T8) that determine the unique cost function, golden ratio scaling, eight-tick cadence, and integer quantization. Formalized in Lean 4, these results provide a machine-verifiable spine yielding a parameter-free derivation chain from pure logic to physical predictions including gravity and the fine structure constant.

Keywords: foundations of physics, logical necessity, machine verification, golden ratio, eight theorems
